Grace Brewster Hopper was an American computer scientist, mathematician, and United States Navy rear admiral. One of the first programmers of the Harvard Mark I computer. Hopper enrolled in Yale University in 1930 after earning her degree from Vassar College in 1928. Before enlisting in the Naval Reserve in 1943, she was a math teacher at Vassar. In 1944, she was promoted to lieutenant and given a job at Harvard University's Bureau of Ordnance Computation Project, where she worked on the Mark I, the first large-scale automatic calculator and an early form of an electronic computer.
